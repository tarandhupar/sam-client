The overall benefits of signing up in the new SAM.gov include streamlined and integrated processes, elimination of data redundancies, and reduced costs while providing improved capability. Entity accounts are discussed in more detail in Registration Organization.

User Sign Up

In the new SAM.gov, any functions are accessible to the general public without logging in, including searching for public information about entities and exclusions. Additional functionality is available to the user with the creation of a SAM Account.

SAM is used by anyone interested in the business of the Federal Government, including:

  • Entities (contractors, federal assistance recipients, and other potential award recipients) who need to register to do business with the government, look for opportunities or assistance programs, or report subcontract information;
  • Government contracting and grants officials responsible for activities with contracts, grants, past performance reporting and suspension and debarment activities;
  • Public users searching for government business information.

Public users that want to save their searches or government users needing access to higher levels of sensitive data must register for a SAM user account. Anyone can create a user account in SAM. A user account provides you access to SAM functionality such as saving search queries, requesting roles for Government employees, and requesting access to non-public information.

The new SAM.gov allows users single sign on access to the previous legacy systems. Once migrated, the user will be able to enter the site with one username and password.

We take the security of your personal information serious. Federal mandate now requires multi-factor authentication when signing in. Along with creating a password and submitting security questions, a one-time access code will be sent to insure the user’s security. The one-time access code will be sent by email each time you sign in.

With the New SAM.gov you can migrate roles from the previous legacy websites without losing any privileges.

Ten Legacy Websites:

  • SAM.gov: System for Award Management
  • FPDS.gov: Federal Procurement Data System – Next Generation
  • FBO.gov: Federal Business Opportunities
  • eSRS.gov: Electronic Subcontracting Reporting System
  • FSRS.gov: Federal Funding Accountability and Transparency Act-Sub award Reporting System
  • CPARS.gov: Contractor Performance Assessment Reporting System
  • FAPIIS.gov: Federal Awardee Performance and Integrity Information System
  • PPIRS.gov: Past Performance Information Retrieval System
  • WDOL.gov: Wage Determination OnLine
  • CFDA.gov: Catalog of Federal Domestic Assistance

If the legacy website is still running, you must go to it and reset your password and try again. Otherwise you must request new access from your administrator.

Create an Individual User Account to perform tasks such as register/update your entity, create and manage exclusion records or to view For Office Use Only (FOUO) level data for entity records.

Information required to sign up:

  • Your first name
  • Your last name
  • Your email address
  • Country (select your option from the dropdown list provided)
  • Phone number
  • Username
  • A password
  • Your security question responses

As part of your SAM account creation, you will be required to prove (“validate”) that you have access to the email address you used.  During the registration process, a link (URL) is sent to your email account that you must click to activate your new SAM account. Click the validation link in the email to activate your user account. If the email link is not hyperlinked (i.e., underlined or appearing in a different color), please copy the validation link and paste it into the browser address bar.

The account you create here will allow you to access them all in one place. After you sign up you can connect existing accounts you may have. This will now give you the ability for single sign on for all accounts.

Click “Forgot Password?”

Enter your username and the email address associated with your account.  This should be the email address that you entered in your SAM account user profile, regardless of whether you currently have access to that email account. 

An email from SAM.gov will be sent to your email account.  Click the link in the email.

Create your new password.  Your new password should be different than your previous ten passwords you have used in SAM.
  • Go to SAM’s homepage (SAM.gov), login with your username and password. You will be directed to your My SAM page.
  • Select the “Existing Entity Registrations” section found in the navigation panel on the left side of the screen.
  • Search for the entity registration that you want to update. If the registration is active, the information will show under Registration Details for Complete Record.
  • Select the Update Entity button. Make the necessary changes, and click “Save and Continue” on each updated page.
  • After you review the entire registration, click “Submit” on the final review page.

Once the account is deleted, all the My Access information is deleted. You will receive an email confirmation that your account and access has been deleted within the next 48 hours.

Recipient organization (business, state or local government, non-profit, etc)

An administrator can invite other administrators or non-administrator users to access/view the entity registration in SAM. Once an administrator is assigned to the registration, all other users will need to gain access either by invitation or by requesting a user role.
  • Manage your current users by clicking “Users List.” You can:
  • View a user’s profile information
  • Give a user additional roles
  • Remove a user’s roles
  • Unlock a locked user’s account
  • Invite users to your entity by clicking “Invite User.”
  • View/delete invitations that you’ve sent by clicking “Pending Sent Invitations.”
  • Approve/reject requests for roles with your entity by clicking “Pending Roles Requests.”
