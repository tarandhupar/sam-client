User Accounts

SAM users can generally be grouped into three types: federal users, award recipients (entity users), and public users. Depending on which type of user you are, you may need to create a user account to access additional features in SAM.

What You Can Do Without An Account

You can search for the following public information without a user account:

  • Federal assistance listings
  • Contract opportunities
  • Contract awards
  • Entity registrations
  • Entity exclusions
  • Federal hierarchy
  • Wage determinations

Sign Up Now

You can now create your user account in SAM by clicking the Sign Up link.

While you won’t be able to access any additional capabilities yet, you can prepare by creating your account now. When the additional features become ready in the upcoming months, you’ll be ready to sign in.

Note: Even if you have an existing account in one of the legacy systems (e.g., SAM.gov, FBO.gov, CFDA.gov) you’ll need to create a new account for beta.SAM.gov. In the future, after all of the individual systems are retired, this account with be your only account for all of our features.

Coming Soon

In the future, federal users for both federal assistance and procurement, as well as agency administrators, will be able to access important functionality with their user accounts.

Take a look at the table below to see which what functionality will one day be available for your user group.

Users Federal Assistance Federal Procurement
Federal users who make and manage awards (assistance coordinators and agency program managers, contracting officers)
  • Create, update, review and approve assistance listings
  • Review FFATA sub-award reports
  • Access non-public entity information
  • Create, publish, modify, and manage contract opportunity notices
  • Add contract award action data via a contract writing system or manual data entry
  • Access reports features based on your assigned role
  • Review small business and FFATA sub-award reports
  • Create and manage past performance evaluations
  • Access non-public entity information
Entity users who receive awards (assistance recipients, contractors)
  • Register as an entity before applying for awards as a prime recipient.
  • Report on sub-awards if you’re a prime awardee and have distributed sub-awards greater than or equal to $25,000.
  • Register as an entity before applying for awards as a prime contractor.
  • Track changes during the post-award process to ensure that workers are receiving appropriate wages.
  • Report on subawards if you’re a prime contractor and have distributed sub-awards greater than $30,000.
Federal users who administer awards (Compliance administrators, agency administrators)
  • View administrative reports to oversee agency compliance
  • Create, delete or modify roles for agency's users
All users (federal users, award recipients, public users)
  • Save search results and detailed record pages
  • View public reports for award data
